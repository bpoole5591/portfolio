About Me

Hello, my name is Brian Poole and I am a Dallas, Texas-based full stack web developer. In my past life I worked in the grocery industry for 15 years and also earned an Associate's Degree in supply chain management. I then re-discovered my adolescent interest in web development and realized that this is my calling. I enrolled myself in DevMountain, a 13-week, fully-immersive web development bootcamp and knew right away that I had made the right choice. I found that I enjoy back-end development, since it satiates my natural curiosity of how things work. Being that I am also accomplishment-driven, I enjoy front-end development as well, so I can view the fruits of my labor.

Programming feels like a natural progression in my career, as I draw parallels between the two fields. The back-end is like the back room in a grocery store -- the ordering and organization of products is akin to making calls/requests to/from a database. Without the back-end and proper structure, there would not be anything to render on the front-end (salesfloor). But in the end, all the user (customer) cares about is that the product is there, how good it looks, and how user-friendly the layout is. Having had extensive experience working with this paradigm gives me an interesting perspective into web development.

In my free time I attend concerts, dog-sit on Rover.com with my wife, design and build things in my garage, and like to see how green my thumb is. I also keep myself challenged by learning new programming languages and frameworks, and picking up cooking techniques from the likes of Gordon Ramsay and Jamie Oliver.
